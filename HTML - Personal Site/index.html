Robert Lewandowski

The most clinical (and arguably the best) striker of the 21st century.

A small boy from the outskirts of Warsaw, Poland had a big dream. Starting from being rejected by Polish lower tier teams, Robert has trained hard to find a place in Znicz Pruszkow in the 2nd and 3rd tier of Polish football, being the top scorer in both divisions and bringing his team up to the highest tier, Ekstraklasa. During the 2009/10 season, Robert has moved to Lech Poznan and carried them to win the Ekstraklasa. After a failed move to Blackburn Rovers, Lewandowski transferred to Borrusia Dortmund, rapidly transforming from a flop signing to a world class striker - with this came a free transfer to the best team in Germany in 2014, cementing his legacy in football during his 8 year-long stay in Bayern Munich.
